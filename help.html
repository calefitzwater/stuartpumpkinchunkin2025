Option 1: Simple Catapult (Easiest)

How it works: Uses a lever arm that rotates around a pivot point. Pull down the arm, release, and it flings the projectile.

Materials needed: Wood boards (2x4s work great), screws, a strong pivot bolt, bungee cords or springs, and something for the counterweight (bucket filled with rocks, sand, or weights).

Tip: The longer the throwing arm compared to the counterweight arm, the farther it will throw.

Example: Simple Catapult Tutorial

Option 2: Trebuchet (More Distance)

How it works: A swinging arm with a sling that releases the projectile. Gravity pulls the counterweight down, which swings the arm up.

Materials needed: Stronger wood frame (needs to support 25 lbs dropping), long arm (4-6 feet), rope or chain for sling, pivot axle, counterweight.

Tip: The release angle is critical. Aim for the sling to release around 45 degrees for maximum distance.

‚úì Arm Length Ratio: For best distance, make your throwing arm 3-4 times longer than the counterweight side.

‚úì Sturdy Base: Build a wide, heavy base so your catapult does not tip over when firing.

‚úì Strong Pivot: Use a metal bolt or rod for the pivot. It needs to handle significant force.

‚úì Smooth Release: Test your release mechanism many times. Consistency leads to better scores.

‚úì Adjustability: Add ways to adjust the counterweight position or release point for tuning.
